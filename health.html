Rising Cases of Dengue in Urban India Spark Health Alert

Kolkata, August 11, 2025: Health authorities across major Indian cities have issued a warning as dengue fever cases have surged sharply this monsoon season. According to the Ministry of Health, reported dengue infections have increased by 40% compared to last year, putting hospitals and clinics on high alert. Dengue fever typically starts with high fever, severe headaches, muscle and joint pain, and rashes. In severe cases, it can lead to dengue haemorrhagic fever, causing internal bleeding and potentially life-threatening complications. Doctors urge the public to seek immediate medical attention if symptoms worsen or if bleeding occurs. Experts attribute the rise to several factors, including: Unseasonal heavy rains leading to water stagnation, creating perfect breeding grounds for the Aedes aegypti mosquito, the primary carrier of dengue. Urban overcrowding and inadequate sanitation in some areas, which exacerbate mosquito proliferation. Increased travel between cities, spreading the virus to new regions. The Central and State Governments have launched intensified mosquito control programs, including fogging operations, community awareness drives, and removal of stagnant water bodies. Hospitals are also increasing readiness to handle severe cases. The Ministry of Health’s spokesperson emphasized, “Prevention is key. Citizens must take personal responsibility by keeping their surroundings clean and using mosquito repellents.” With dengue posing a renewed threat every monsoon, experts say sustained community action combined with government efforts is essential to curb the spread. As the season progresses, vigilance and prompt medical care remain the best defence. Use mosquito nets and repellents, especially during dawn and dusk when mosquitoes are most active. Avoid water accumulation around homes and public places. Wear full-sleeved clothing during peak mosquito hours.
