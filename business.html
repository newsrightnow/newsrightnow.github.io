Google Refutes Claims That AI Overviews Are Hurting Website Traffic

Kolkata, 11 August, 2025 — Google has officially denied recent concerns suggesting that its AI-generated “overview” snippets are causing a drop in traffic to original websites. In a new report released this week, Google emphasized that these AI summaries, designed to provide quick, helpful answers directly on search results pages, do not replace or reduce visits to the full content on websites. Over the past few months, many website owners and digital publishers expressed worry that Google's AI-powered answers might reduce clicks to their pages, potentially affecting ad revenue and user engagement. Critics argued that by showing detailed summaries directly on Google’s search page, users would find less need to visit the source sites. Google’s latest data suggests otherwise. The company states that AI overviews are intended to supplement, not substitute, detailed website content. They claim most users still click through to the original sources to explore further. A Google spokesperson said, “Our goal is to help users find answers faster while supporting publishers by driving meaningful traffic to their sites.” While the report reassures some publishers, skepticism remains among others who argue the impact may vary by industry and type of content. Experts suggest that ongoing monitoring and transparent data sharing from Google will be key to resolving these concerns in the evolving AI search landscape.
