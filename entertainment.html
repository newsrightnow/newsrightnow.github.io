Bengali Films to Secure 50% Prime-Time Slots in State Theatres

By Subhanita Dasgupta

Kolkata, August 30, 2025 : In a significant move to bolster the local film industry, the Federation of Cine Technicians and Workers of Eastern India (FCTWEI) has announced that 50% of prime-time theatre slots in West Bengal will be reserved for Bengali films. This initiative, supported by the Eastern India Motion Pictures Association (EIMPA) and various production companies, aims to address concerns about the limited visibility of Bengali cinema in theatres.

The decision follows a meeting convened by state minister Aroop Biswas, attended by over 40 representatives from the Tollywood industry, including filmmakers, distributors, and actors. Including Dev, Rituparna Sengupta etc. Discussions centered on ensuring that Bengali films receive priority in theatres, securing better show timings and extended run times. A panel has been formed to synchronize release schedules, particularly during major festivals like Durga Puja, Eid, and Christmas, to prevent overcrowding and conflicting schedules.

FCTWEI president Swarup Biswas emphasized the importance of technicians in Bengali cinema and the Federation's role in making these films accessible to audiences. The 50% prime-time allocation supplements a prior mandate requiring 120 screenings of Bengali films annually, which were often given non-ideal time slots. While no specific directive exists for independent films, exhibitors can choose the films. Additionally, a committee has been formed to manage the release of major films during key festival periods such as Durga Puja, Eid, and Christmas to avoid release clashes and support distributors and exhibitors.

This move is seen as a response to criticism of the Federation's role in supporting the Bengali film industry and is expected to enhance the visibility and commercial success of Bengali cinema in the state.
